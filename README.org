** Concept Overview
:tag:idea:premise:ironic-domesticity:
Spouse Points is a tongue-in-cheek relationship utility designed to gamify appreciation and recognition within long-term partnerships. It plays on the unspoken economy of emotional labor and domestic effort, particularly among couples with children and full-time careers. It is not a serious scorekeeping tool (god no), but rather a comedic and affectionate mechanism to prompt awareness and gratitude.

** Generational Critique
:tag:generational-analysis:cultural-commentary:

*** Millennial Context
Millennials (b. ~1981-1996) are perfectly positioned for ironic detachment *and* desperate emotional sincerity. Raised on participation trophies and traumatized by the 2008 crash, they are:
- Emotionally self-aware but avoidant.
- Technologically fluent, but burnt out by the “app-ification” of everything.
- Obsessed with “adulting,” and unable to maintain systems that feel like work.

Spouse Points fits their need for *low-stakes engagement* and *performative intimacy*. They’ll download it. They’ll mock it. They’ll use it more than they admit.

*** Gen Z Avoidance
Gen Z (b. ~1997-2012) may find the concept cringe and overly structured, preferring vibes over systems. Points imply rules. Rules imply stress. They’re out—unless it becomes a meme.

*** Gen X Indifference
Gen X (b. ~1965-1980) won’t use this. They invented sarcasm and would rather go bowling. Targeting them would be like handing a Tamagotchi to a wolf.

** MVP Development Strategy
:tag:product:strategy:minimalism:

*** Phase 0: Validation
- Launch a Carrd/Notion explainer site with email capture.
- Offer a Notion-based “template relationship ledger” as proof of concept.
- Post fake point logs (e.g. “+2 points for not sighing while folding towels”) to Instagram/Twitter to test shareability.

*** Phase 1: Prototype Humor Engine
- Create a daily/weekly email or Telegram bot that suggests fake points.
- Add surprise mechanics: “Random Appreciation Day – Send 3 points NOW.”
- Explore passive UI like a screensaver widget or simple browser extension.

*** Phase 2: Monetize the Irony
- Physical card deck version for couples who hate screens but love bits.
- Optional upgrade: cute pixel-based “Appreciation Pet” that reacts to point totals.
- Subscription for personalized prompts and confessional features.

** Risks & Anti-Features
:tag:warning:satire:emotional-landmines:
- Emotional labor backlash (“I don’t want to gamify my marriage, Susan.”)
- Scorekeeping spiral leading to genuine resentment.
- High potential for people taking a joke app seriously and ending therapy.

** North Star Theme
:tag:purpose:soul:
> Appreciation should not require a scoreboard—but maybe a little scoreboard helps us laugh while we learn to notice each other better.
